Homework 1

Deadline:

hw1

Instructions: Make a map of the area where you grew up (if you are comfortable sharing this information, otherwise make a map of a different area of your interest). You will have to find (online) a vector data set for your county, state or country, and use this to make the map.

  1. Do an online search for GIS data in the neighborhood, county, state or country that you choose. Look first for the neighborhood, and if you don't find data at that scale, then search at the county/city level, and if you don't find data at that scale, then search at the state and finally at the country or world level (eg: UN or FAO database).
  2. Create a map displaying the base map (as described in lecture) and the layers of data that you found. Change the symbols to make the map clearer and to practice using symbology.
  3. Answer the following questions:
    • What was the finest scale of data that you found?
    • List up to ten types of layers that you found.
    • In what formats could the data be downloaded?
    • What problems, if any, did you encounter while trying to use a particular dataset?
- -

Where can I find data?

A good starting point is going to your favorite browser and search engine and entering the name of your neighborhood, county or state, and add the keywords "shapefile" or "geojson" or "GIS layers" or "GIS data."

You can also go to your city or county's official webpage and look for GIS data or mapping data.
