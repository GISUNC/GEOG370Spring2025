Homework 3: World Projection Maps

Deadline:

hw3

The objective of this homework is for you to get familiarized with projections. You will practice using the projection-on-the-fly feature in QGIS that can display the data in different projections. You will also generate vector and raster files in new projections.  

 

Watch the following videos related to projections to find out how to complete the following tasks. 

 
  1. Select a location of interest (LOI) in the south of the equator and east of the prime meridian.  
  2. Find the latitude and longitude of the LOI. 
  3. Create the following layers.   
  • A geojson file with the LOI in 4326   
  • A geojson file with the LOI in 3857 
  • A geojson file with the LOI in the appropriate UTM zone.  
  • A geojson graticule in 4326 and the appropriate UTM zone for the LOI.  
  • A Natural Earth Cross Blended Hypso with Shaded Relief and Water reprojected to an orthographic projection with the center on the LOI and a pixel size of 25,000 (in the video I make it at a  pixel size of 10,000, but I want to keep your file sizes smaller).  

 

You will submit a zip file that contains: 

  1. The reprojected raster (this should be less than 2MB in size), 
  2. the geoJSON point in the appropriate UTM* 
  3. the geoJSON graticule in the appropriate UTM  
  4. and a csv table with the following information. 

  

The table should look like this. 

 

Name, x, y, EPSG, notes 

Old_well, -79.05123, 35.91206, 4326, units are in degrees. 

Old_well, -8799942.670181952416897, 4288527.700660364702344, 3857, units are in meters with x values negative since the location is west of the prime meridian.  

Old_well, 675843.467835035640746, 3975949.105584203265607, 32617, units are in meters and are all positive. 

 

*(Please do not submit the point geoJSONs in 4326 or 3857)
